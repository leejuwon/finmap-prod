## 1. Introduction  
Many beginner investors wonder why annual and monthly compounding produce different results.  
The reason is simple: **the more often interest is applied, the faster the balance grows**.  
This small difference becomes noticeable when comparing long-term results or goal timelines.

## FAQ  

### Q1. Is monthly compounding always better?  
Not always. Over short periods, the difference is minimal. It matters mainly in long-term investing.

### Q2. Do any financial products use annual compounding?  
Yes. Certain savings accounts and insurance products calculate interest annually.

### Q3. Do ETFs use monthly compounding?  
Since contributions happen monthly, the practical effect is similar to monthly compounding.
